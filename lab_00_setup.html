Copilot Setup & Access Overview

This section provides an overview of how to access Copilot in Microsoft 365 and how it connects to your work.

1. Login

To use Copilot in Microsoft 365:

  1. Go to office.com
  2. Sign in with your work or school account
  3. Ensure you have a Microsoft 365 Copilot license assigned

Once signed in, Copilot becomes available across supported apps.

2. Where you can access Copilot

Copilot is integrated across Microsoft 365 — you can access it in multiple places depending on your task.

In the browser (Copilot Chat)

Accessible from the Microsoft 365 homepage. Use it to:

  • ask questions and explore ideas
  • combine work data + web information
  • prepare before working in documents or presentations

Inside apps

Copilot works directly where your work happens.

  • Word
    Ask Copilot → analyze and discuss the document
    Edit with Copilot → rewrite, expand, improve content
  • PowerPoint
    Create presentations from documents | Improve slides, add notes, refine messaging
  • Excel
    Analyze data | Generate insights, summaries, formulas
  • Outlook
    Summarize email threads | Draft replies | Identify key actions
  • Teams
    Summarize chats and meetings | Generate meeting recaps | Help track decisions and follow-ups | Ask questions about conversations and shared content

Copilot Agents (simple/custom agents)

Copilot can also be extended with agents:

  • Built for specific topics or tasks
  • Can use your organization's documents as knowledge
  • Provide focused, consistent answers

Examples: answering internal policy questions, supporting sales or customer inquiries, helping with onboarding or training content.

3. How Copilot connects to your data

Copilot is powered by Microsoft Graph. Microsoft Graph connects:

  • Emails (Outlook)
  • Files (Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Meetings and chats (Teams)
  • Calendar information

This allows Copilot to understand your work context and provide relevant responses.

4. Connection to OneDrive and SharePoint

Your files are stored in:

  • OneDrive → personal work files
  • SharePoint → team and shared files

Copilot uses these locations as its data source. This means:

  • it works with your actual documents
  • it reflects what you and your team are working on
  • results depend on access and permissions

5. Work and Web grounding

Copilot can use two types of information:

  • Work: your files, emails and conversations, internal context
  • Web: external information, market trends, general knowledge

6. Simple way to understand Copilot

  • Inside apps → create and edit content
  • In chat → explore, analyze, and decide
  • With Graph → uses your work context
  • With Web → adds external knowledge
